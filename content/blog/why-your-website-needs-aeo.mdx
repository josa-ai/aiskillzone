## Search Is Changing, and Your Website Needs to Keep Up

For years, SEO has been the gold standard for getting found online. You optimize your pages with keywords, build backlinks, and hope Google ranks you on page one. That strategy still matters, but a new layer has entered the game: AI-powered search engines. Tools like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity are changing how people find and choose businesses. If your website is not optimized for these AI engines, you are becoming invisible to a growing number of potential customers.

## What Is AEO?

AEO stands for AI Engine Optimization. While SEO focuses on ranking in traditional search engine results pages, AEO focuses on making your website easy for AI systems to understand, summarize, and recommend. When someone asks an AI assistant to find a plumber in Lakeland or the best cleaning service in Winter Haven, the AI pulls from structured, well-organized content to form its answer. If your website is not structured in a way that AI can parse, you will not be part of that answer.

## How AI Search Engines Work Differently

Traditional search engines crawl pages and rank them based on relevance, authority, and technical factors. AI search engines do something extra: they read, interpret, and synthesize content to generate direct answers. This means your content needs to be clear, factual, and structured so that an AI can extract meaningful information without guessing.

## Structured Data and Schema Markup

One of the most important tools in your AEO toolkit is structured data, often implemented through schema markup. This is code added to your website that explicitly tells search engines and AI what your business does, where you are located, what services you offer, your hours of operation, and more. Think of it as a cheat sheet for AI. Without it, AI has to infer details from your page copy, and it often gets things wrong or skips you entirely.

## Why Small Businesses Need to Act Now

Large companies already have teams working on AEO. Small businesses have an opportunity to get ahead of local competitors by acting early. Adding structured data, writing clear and direct content, organizing your site with logical headings, and maintaining accurate business information across the web are all steps you can take today.

## The Bottom Line

SEO is not dead, but it is no longer enough on its own. AEO is the next evolution, and the businesses that adapt first will capture the traffic that others miss. If your website was built more than a year or two ago, it is almost certainly not optimized for AI search. Now is the time to fix that.
